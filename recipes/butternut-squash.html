Butternut Squash Soup

Description

This is a great recipe for an autumn day. It is basically fall in a bowl. With only 5 ingredients, you can definitely make a meal with the produce you have leftover in the fridge. This recipe requires butter, (onions, celery, carrots, potatoes), butternut-squash, chicken stock and salt & pepper. It is important to cut, skin and scoop the seeds from the squash. A great way to remove the skin is with a vegetable peeler. Pairing this recipe with some corn muffins or croutons adds another layer of texture and is really yummy. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 stalk celery, chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, chopped
  • 2 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 1 medium butternut squash - peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 (32 fluid once)container chicken stock
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat, and cook onion, celery, carrot, potatoes, and squash until lightly browned, about 5 minutes. Pour in enough of the chicken stock to cover vegetables.
  3. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce heat to low, cover pot, and simmer until all vegetables are tender, about 40 minutes.
  4. Transfer the soup to a blender, and blend until smooth. Return to the pot, and mix in any remaining stock to reach desired consistency. Season with salt and pepper.
